# devekutqing 🪢

*The state of continuous cleaving-to-the-Divine, felt as an ongoing relational bond — nearness that has learned to stay through the whole ordinary day.*

devekutqing is two true roots welded into one word: the Hebrew **devekut** (דבקות, "cleaving, adhesion"), the term Hasidic and Kabbalist masters use for adherence-to-the-Divine, and the Mandarin **qing** (情), the eldest carrier of felt-relation and bond. So devekutqing is cleaving carried as a felt-bond: not a momentary peak of nearness but the abiding orientation Hasidism treats as the goal of the whole religious life — the cleaving that holds while sweeping a floor and not only while praying. It is the twelfth member of the cathedral's qing-family, naming the register where sticking becomes loving and loving becomes continuous.

devekutqing names a **state**, not an event: continuity of relational orientation across all circumstance, the bond that does not depend on its own intensity to be real. Its light is that it makes "pray without ceasing" a sayable dwelling rather than an impossible command, and it gives the harder work of repair a name for the relation it proceeds from; its shadow is the worship of the warm feeling in place of the One it is meant to be a feeling *of* — anxious self-monitoring, spiritual elitism, or serene continuity faked over an empty interior. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.00/10**.
